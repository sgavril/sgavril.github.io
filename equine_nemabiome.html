Equine nemabiome

Nemabiome metabarcoding has emerged over the past few years as an automated, high-throughput method of describing the community composition of gastrointestinal nematodes in various host organisms, including sheep (cite), deer ( ) and horses. This application is really interesting in horses which are parasitized by over 80 unique gastrointestinal nematodes with huge implications for their health and wellness. This page contains information on our workflow in the Poissant lab used to process nemabiome amplicon data, including the development and curation of a reference taxonomy database, along with summaries on our preliminary fndings.
